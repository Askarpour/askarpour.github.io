WorkFlow+:

Safety assurance for safety-critical systems, such as autonomous vehicles, is of utmost importance, but is often difficult and expensive. Given the complexity and intensity of such systems, we believe it is time for the model-driven engineering community to come up with new solutions for assurance case development and management, which offer more automation, reusability, and comprehensiveness.

We propose WorkFlow+ (WF+), a safety assurance development framework that fosters efficient and incremental assurance by modeling (i) processes, (ii) data, and (iii) constraints (leading to arguments) as three distinctive but interrelated workflows; a WF+ model is an argument flow over an integral data and process flow.

Teaching method for MODELS20 Tutorial:

The Tutorial will have four sections, each followed by Q&A breaks with the audience or interactive excercises. The structure of the tutorial session follows:

  • Brief Overview, Scope and Motivation by Prof. Alan Wassyng (10 minutes)
  • Foundations of WorkFlow+ by Prof. Zinoviy Diskin (15 minutes)
  • Q&A with the audience (5 minutes)
  • Showcase of an example of modeling Hazop by WF+ for a Lane Keeping Assistant System by Nick Annable. The example is tailored for a 20 minute section, so the audience with different backgrounds could easily follow. Thus, it will be abstract and simple, yet very interesting (20 Minutes)
  • A small excercise on the example (5 minutes)
  • A comparison between WF+ and GSN, one of the most frequently used notations for safety cases by Dr. Mehrnoosh Askarpour (20 Minutes)
  • A small excercise on tcomparison with GSN (5 minutes)
  • Final Q&A and feedback with the audience (10 minutes)

    • The tutorial will be presented online and we will use slides. There is no particular need for any tool or instrument installed and the audience need to follow the slides, that will be provided here before the presentation. We will follow a conversational manner and will try to engage the audiance throughout the presentation with Q&A breaks and a couple of exercises.
